Our Story

Six Decades of
Service Above Self

The Rotary Club of Lagos holds the distinction of being the oldest active Rotary Club in Nigeria. Chartered on 30 May 1961, just three months after Rotary first entered the country through Kano, our club has spent over six decades building fellowship, driving community impact, and shaping leaders who serve with purpose.

We are part of Rotary International District 9112 — formally created on 1 July 2024 from the split of District 9110 — and we operate under the global Rotary motto: "Service Above Self." Our members are business and professional leaders united by a shared commitment to humanitarian service.

Timeline

Our History, Chapter
by Chapter

22 February 1905

Rotary International Founded

Paul P. Harris and three friends found the first Rotary club in Chicago, sharing fellowship among entrepreneurs. The wheel begins to turn.

28 April 1961

Rotary Enters Nigeria

The Rotary Club of Kano is chartered — the first in Nigeria, the 32nd nation to receive Rotary in Africa. Nigeria becomes part of District 210, alongside 14 West African countries.

30 May 1961

Rotary Club of Lagos Chartered

Just weeks after Kano, the Rotary Club of Lagos receives its charter — becoming the second Rotary club in Nigeria and the foundation upon which decades of impact would be built.

1972–73

Nigeria's First District Governor

Rotarian Anofi Guobadia of the Rotary Club of Ikeja becomes Nigeria's first District Governor for District 210, marking a new era of local leadership in Rotary.

1982–83

District 911 Created

Nigeria becomes large enough for its own district. PDG Julius Adelusi-Adeluyi leads the newly created all-Nigerian District 911, criss-crossing the country to spread the message of Rotary and charter 31 new clubs.

1986

Redistricting — Four Nigerian Districts

Sustained growth leads to redistricting. Nigeria begins the Rotary year with four districts: 911, 912, 913, and 914 — each serving distinct regions of the country.

2003–04

Nigerian Leads Rotary International

Jonathan Babatunde Majiyagbe, S.A.N., OFR — a member of the Rotary Club of Kano — serves as President of Rotary International, a proud moment for Nigerian Rotarians.

1 July 2024

District 9112 Formed

Rotary International approves the split of District 9110 into Districts 9111 and 9112. The Rotary Club of Lagos becomes part of the newly created District 9112, covering clubs across Lagos and surrounding areas.

2025–26

President Toki Mabogunje's Tenure

The club continues to grow in impact and membership under the leadership of President Toki Mabogunje, setting the stage for the historic 66th chapter.

3 July 2026

66th President Installed

Rotarian Olutoyin Mobisola Odulate is installed as the 66th President of the Rotary Club of Lagos — the daughter of the club's 16th President, Past President Dr. Dele Odulate. A legacy of service continues.
